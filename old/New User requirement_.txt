1. Platform Overview

o	Customers seeking renovation/decoration services.

o	Reduce information asymmetry in the renovation industry.

4. Due Diligence & Compliance Engine
4.1 Real Time BD Licence Verification
•	Integration with BD’s “註冊名單搜尋系統”: 
o	Automatic lookup by company name or registration number.
o	Verification of: 
	Licence class (一級/二級/三級)
	Categories (A–G)
	Responsible person
	Validity period
o	Automated periodic re checks.
•	Enforcement: 
o	Only qualified companies may bid on regulated works. 
o	System blocks non qualified companies automatically.
4.2 TransUnion Credit Assessment (PDPO Compliant)
•	User authorization workflow: 
o	When applying for financing or bidding on certain projects, companies are shown: 
	Purpose of credit assessment.
	Types of data collected (e.g., credit score band, credit history length).
	How the data improves their credibility and loan eligibility.
•	Explicit consent: 
o	Companies must provide written, explicit consent via web confirmation.
o	Consent covers sharing necessary data with TransUnion.
•	Data minimization: 
o	Platform receives only encrypted, anonymized indicators, such as: 
	Credit score band.
	Credit history length.
	Risk classification.
o	No raw credit reports or detailed credit history.
•	Integration into risk model: 
o	TransUnion indicators feed into the “財務健康評分” model.
o	Combined with project history, dispute history, and BD licence status.
4.3 Multi Source Risk Assessment
•	Data sources: 
o	BD licence verification.
o	TransUnion credit indicators.
o	Company Registry (BR status, incorporation date).
o	Court judgment records (civil disputes, construction claims).
o	Media and social sentiment analysis (NLP).
•	Output: 
o	Composite “可信度指數” displayed to customers.
o	Used to filter out high risk companies.

5. MSO Licensing & Regulatory Framework
5.1 MSO Licence Requirement
•	DecoFinance’s escrow model qualifies as money service business because: 
o	Platform receives customer funds.
o	Funds are transferred to third party decoration companies.
•	Under Hong Kong law: 
o	Money service operators must obtain an MSO licence.
o	Operating without a licence is a criminal offence.
5.2 MSO Compliance Obligations
•	AML/CFT framework required under AMLO: 
o	Customer Due Diligence (CDD): 
	Verify identity of customers and decoration companies.
	Understand nature and purpose of transactions.
o	Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD): 
	For high risk cases (PEPs, unusual transactions).
o	Ongoing Monitoring: 
	Detect unusual patterns (e.g., sudden large payments).
o	Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR): 
	Mandatory reporting to JFIU.
o	Record keeping: 
	Retain transaction and identity records for 7 years.
•	Fit and proper requirements: 
o	Directors and shareholders must pass integrity checks.
5.3 Escrow Legal Structure
•	Recommended structure: 
o	Three party agreement between customer, contractor, and platform.
o	Funds held in bank managed segregated account.
o	Platform acts as payment instruction agent, not trustee.
•	Benefits: 
o	Avoids trust law obligations.
o	Reduces regulatory burden.
o	Ensures transparency and fund safety.

7. Milestone Based Payment Escrow
7.1 Funding
•	Customer deposits funds into bank managed segregated account.
•	Platform never holds funds directly.
7.2 Recommended Disbursement Logic
Stage	%	Trigger
Stage 1	30%	Contract signed + company passes BD/TransUnion checks
Stage 2	30%	Structural/major works completed + evidence uploaded
Stage 3	35%	Final completion + independent inspection or customer acceptance
Retention	5%	Released after 6 month defect liability period
7.3 Dispute Freeze Logic
•	When dispute is filed: 
o	All pending payments are frozen.
o	Evidence collected via web uploads.
o	Third party mediator or expert may be engaged.
•	Platform remains neutral: 
o	Does not act as arbitrator.
o	Executes final decision per dispute resolution rules.
________________________________________
8. Dispute Resolution Framework
•	Step 1: Dispute submission 
o	Customer or contractor submits dispute via web portal.
o	Uploads photos, videos, communication logs.
•	Step 2: Evidence freeze 
o	Pending payments frozen in segregated account.
•	Step 3: Internal review 
o	Platform compliance team reviews basic facts.
•	Step 4: Third party involvement 
o	For technical disputes: 
	Independent inspector.
	Mediation centre.
•	Step 5: Final decision 
o	Based on evidence + expert report.
o	Platform issues payment instruction accordingly.
•	Step 6: Record archiving 
o	Dispute outcome stored permanently.
o	Impacts company’s “可信度指數”.
